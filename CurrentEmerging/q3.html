How Do They Work

Material Extrusion

Also known as MEX, during this 3D printing process a plastic filament is extruded through a nozzle. It is then cooled and solidified into the desired object.

Vat Polymerization

Also known as resin 3D printing, this method uses a light source to solidify photopolymer resin in a vat. The light is directed to specific areas of the liquid plastic, causing it to solidify. After printing, the object is cleaned to remove excess resin.

Powder Bed Fusion

This process uses thermal energy to melt particles such as plastic, metal, or ceramic. A blade spreads powdered material over the print bed, and energy—usually from a laser—melts it layer by layer until the object is complete.

Material Jetting

Small droplets of material are deposited and hardened on a build plate. This process uses photopolymers or wax droplets that cure when exposed to light. The object is built layer by layer.

Binder Jetting

Powdered materials like metal, plastic, ceramic, or wood are spread in thin layers by a roller. An inkjet nozzle passes over the build, depositing a binding agent. This process is repeated until the object is complete.

Directed Energy Deposition

One of the broadest categories of 3D printing, this method uses strong energy sources like lasers or electron beams to melt metal material. The melted material is deposited and solidified layer by layer.

Sheet Lamination

This process involves cutting sheets into the desired shape using a blade or laser, then bonding them layer by layer until a 3D object is formed.
