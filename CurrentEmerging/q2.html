3D Printing

Applications

What applications are there for 3D printing?

The Medical Industry

3D printing has become quite a game changer in the medical industry, with a wide variety of applications in the space.

Fitted Dental Implants 3D printers can make exact-fit dental implants using CT scans of the patient and CAD modelling. This can greatly improve comfort for the patient as well as give better functionality.
(Raise3D, 2024)
Prosthetics Similarly to dental implants, prosthetics can be greatly improved through 3d printing by taking precise measurements of the patient and using CAD models to quickly produce precise fitting proesthic limbs. It also allows for greater patient customization due to how easy it is to make changes to the prosthetic through this method.
(Naoum, 2025)
Surgical Planning & Testing 3D printing allows for quite the improvement in surgery preparedness by allowing surgeons and research teams to use diagnostic imaging data to recreate a patients organs in 3D printed form. This allows the surgeons to develop better strategies pre-op, creating safer, more efficient, and more accurate procudures.
(Raise3D, 2024)
Drug Administering Devices Instruments for delivering various drug doses can be made using 3D printing, allowing for the quick creation of the parts requiered to precisely administer very specific doses of medications.
(Raise3D, 2024)

Defense and Aerospace Industries

When it comes to aerospace and defense, 3D printing has allowing for impressive weight reduction of many different parts, as well as significant reductions of production costs.

Common Benefits Across the board 3D printing has allowed for significant weight reduction, a critical factor in aircraft and spacecraft design. It also reduces production costs, part lead times, and waste.
(Raise3D, 2024)
Aircraft Manufacturing Aircraft from many companies these days, particularily Airbus, have thousands of 3D printed components in them. These parts vary from simple cabin components, all the way to critical engine components.
(Raise3D, 2024)
Space Travel Rocket payloads can cost well upwards of $10,000 per pound of weight (Ward, 2024). This means that saving every single possible gram is of critical importance. 3D printing allows for the fast creation of extremely lightweight components made of a variety of materials, saving pounds, and therefore potentially tens of thousands of dollars per launch.
(Raise3D, 2024)

Personal Use

3D printing has seen massive use by individuals for DIY, hobbies, and personal projects. It makes it much easier for people with less hands-on manufacturing experience to produce their own items quickly and for very little cost. These can be parts for devices, decorations, toys, paperweights, art pieces, or just about anything you can think of. 3D printers range in price from only a few hundred dollars up to many thousands of dollars, so the cost of entry isn't high for a lower-end printer. This makes it possible for it to be far cheaper and easier than making these things using expensive power tools and materials.
